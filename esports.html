If you're new to the concept of eSports, let me tell you about what is truly going on behind the minds of the players, because they are not simply pressing buttons. The amount of mental processes going on in one's mind while competing in a video game tournament is comprable to that of a very fast-paced version of chess. This is because to defeat an opponent in, say, a fighting game, you must be able to use your character's strengths, abuse the opponent's character weakness, and make sure you keep track of what options they are choosing in specific situations so that you can punish them when you see them later on. These processes are the reason why eSports has its name, not because of the often percieved difficulty in inputting very specific actions onto a controller but the underlying mental difficulty that can be compared to the physical difficulty found in regular sports.
